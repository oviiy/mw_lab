Backtest

Monthly rebalance. Long the Value/Growth spread when the total score is above its own 24-month trailing median, short it (i.e. long Growth) otherwise; 5 bps cost per switch. Compared to buy&hold Value spread.
Honest limitations. (1) Baskets are a proxy for Russell 1000 Value/Growth (IWD/IWF ETF symbols blocked on FMP free), built from today's constituents held fixed over the whole window — so the deep history carries survivorship/composition bias (e.g. NVDA's current weight applied back when it was tiny). (2) Weights & windows are seeded from the original model's proportions and conventional defaults — not optimized on this data, deliberately, to avoid overfitting a ~10y sample. (3) The signal engine is causal (trailing MAs/z-scores only). Treat as a research illustration, not audited performance.

Walk-forward cross-validation

The ~10y sample split into contiguous out-of-sample sub-periods. Consistent excess across every fold = robust; one lucky fold = fragile. No parameters are fit, so each fold is a pure hold-out of the same causal signal (not train/test optimization).
